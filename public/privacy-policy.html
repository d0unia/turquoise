Privacy Policy

Effective date: 24 May 2026 · Last updated: 24 May 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Turquoise.live (“we”, “us”) collects, uses, and protects information in connection with Turquoise (the “Service”), a content-intelligence tool that measures the quality of attention earned by published content and produces analysis to guide future content.

1. Who this applies to

Turquoise is an invite-only tool used by our team and authorized members of our organization. It is not a consumer product and is not directed to the general public. If you have been invited to use the Service, this policy governs the information processed about you and about the content and accounts you connect.

2. Information we collect

2.1 Information you provide

  • Account information — your name and email address, used to send a sign-in link and to manage organization membership and role. Sign-up is invite-only.
  • Content you log — titles, published URLs, dates, channels, projects, and optional notes about content you choose to track.
  • Configuration — projects, scoring settings, and connected-account details.

2.2 Information from services you connect

When you authorize a connection, we retrieve data needed to score and analyze your content. We retrieve aggregate engagement metrics and post metadata — for example impressions, reactions, comments, shares, saves, link clicks, email opens, and read time. We do not collect or store the names or profiles of the individual people who engaged with your content.

2.3 Information collected automatically

  • Authentication cookies — to keep you signed in.
  • Local storage — small preferences such as your selected project.
  • Server and security logs — standard technical logs (e.g. timestamps, error information) kept by our hosting and infrastructure providers.

3. How we use information

  • To compute attention scores for your content from the metrics described above.
  • To generate analysis and content briefs that summarize what is and is not working.
  • To operate, secure, maintain, and improve the Service.
  • To communicate with you about the Service (e.g. sign-in links, important notices).

We do not sell personal information, and we do not use it for advertising.

4. AI processing

To produce analysis and briefs, content metadata and aggregate metrics are sent to our AI provider (Anthropic) via its API and processed to return structured results. This data is used to provide the Service’s analysis features. We do not send the personal data of individuals who engaged with your content for this purpose.

5. LinkedIn data

Where you connect LinkedIn, we use the LinkedIn API solely to: (a) publish posts that you or your organization have authorized, and (b) retrieve aggregate analytics for those posts. Our use of LinkedIn data complies with the LinkedIn API Terms of Use and the LinkedIn Platform Guidelines. We store only the aggregate metrics and the access credentials needed to operate the connection; access tokens are held securely and refreshed as required. You can revoke access at any time from your LinkedIn account settings or by contacting us, after which we stop retrieving data and delete the stored credentials.

6. Cookies and local storage

We use strictly necessary cookies for authentication and small local-storage values for interface preferences. We do not use third-party advertising or cross-site tracking cookies.

8. How we share information

We share information only with the service providers above, to the extent necessary to operate the Service; when required by law or to protect rights and safety; or in connection with a business transfer (e.g. merger or acquisition), subject to this policy. We do not sell personal information.

9. Data retention

We keep content and metrics only as long as needed for the Service’s analysis function; older tracked items are automatically archived on a rolling basis. Account information is retained while your access is active and deleted on request or within a reasonable period after your access ends, unless we must retain it to meet legal obligations.

10. Security

We use reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect information, including encryption in transit, access controls and row-level data isolation, and restricting access to authorized members. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

11. International transfers

Our providers may process information in countries other than yours. Where required, we rely on appropriate safeguards (such as standard contractual clauses) for such transfers.

12. Your rights

Depending on your location (e.g. under the GDPR or similar laws), you may have the right to access, correct, delete, or port your personal data, to restrict or object to processing, and to withdraw consent. You may also lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority. To exercise any of these rights, contact us at hello@turquoise.live.

13. Children’s privacy

The Service is for business use by authorized adults and is not directed to children. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

14. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. We will revise the “Last updated” date above and, where appropriate, notify you of material changes.
